Re-teach · Campaign Architecture

I Taught This Wrong. Here's the Fix.

Three separate campaigns — not three ad sets inside one. Audience exclusions are why.

✕ What I taught before
1 Campaign · 3 Ad Sets
Structure
Campaign · "reThought Q1"
Ad Set 1 · Cold
Ad Set 2 · Warm
Ad Set 3 · Retarget
  • Same person can see all 3 ads — exclusions don't cascade
  • Learning phase shared across all 3 stages
  • One budget pool — CBO may starve your warm
  • Kill one stage → risk killing the others
✓ The right way
3 Separate Campaigns
Structure
Campaign 1 · Cold / AIDA
Campaign 2 · Warm / PAS+FAB
Campaign 3 · Retarget / IDCA
  • Exclusions cascade cleanly: cold → warm → retarget
  • Each stage gets its own learning phase
  • Per-campaign budget = no surprise spend shifts
  • Kill or scale one without touching the other two

Each Campaign Owns Its Audience + Exclusions

Cold builds warm. Warm feeds retarget. Retarget excludes converters. The math is the whole game.

Campaign 01 · Cold
AIDA
30-sec talking-head video. Comment-to-DM via ManyChat.
Include
Broad metros (Milwaukee + Orlando), 25-mi radius. Lookalikes once you have an engaged base.
Exclude
Page engagers (last 90 days). Video viewers 75%+ (last 30 days).
Campaign 02 · Warm
PAS + FAB
5-card carousel + 60-sec FAB video. Calendly booking or DM.
Include
Page engagers (90d). Video viewers 75%+ (30d). IG/FB messengers (90d).
Exclude
Already booked Calendly. Already converted (custom event).
Campaign 03 · Retarget
IDCA
15-sec direct-to-camera. Frequency cap 2/week. Dual CTA.
Include

Exclude
Booked. Converted. Already in client pipeline.
Cold spend builds the Warm audience automatically.
Warm engagement feeds the Retarget audience.
Converters get excluded from all 3 — you stop paying for them.

Middle of Funnel · Warm Audience

Pick the Framework Based on Their Headspace

Warm audiences split two ways. PAS for pain-aware. FAB for comparing.

Framework · Pain-Aware
PAS
Use when they already feel the problem.
  • Problem · name it directly
  • Agitate · twist the knife, show the cost
  • Solution · present your offer
Format: 5-card carousel · single image
Framework · Comparing Options
FAB
Use when they're shopping and want to compare.
  • Feature · what it is
  • Advantage · why it beats the alternative
  • Benefit · what it means for them
Format: 60-sec talking-head · feature breakdown
Pedro's avatar feels both. Pain when they think about their last broker. Comparison when they look at other brokerages. Run both.

Retargeting · Re-engagement

IDCA — for People Who Already Saw You

They watched. They visited. They didn't act. IDCA acknowledges that and breaks the pattern.

I
Identify
Name them by behavior. "You watched the video and didn't book."
D
Disrupt
Break the assumption. Usually they expect a pitch — give them the opposite.
C
Compel
Give the real reason to act now. Loss-frame works here.
A
Act
Make the next step tiny. One tap. Lowest possible friction.
AIDA assumes they don't know you. IDCA assumes they do — and need a different push.

Follow-Up · The Conversation Layer

SCIPAB — the Framework for Texts & Emails

Six pieces. The whole structure assumes the conversation is the bridge — not the ad.

S
Situation
Name where they are right now — the moment they raised their hand.
C
Complication
Acknowledge the friction. The thing in their way.
I
Implication
Point at the cost of not fixing it — usually time, money, or lost momentum.
P
Position
Place yourself as the person who solves exactly that.
A
Action
Ask one small thing. Reply with a number. Pick one of four words.
B
Benefit
Name what they get for taking the small action.
