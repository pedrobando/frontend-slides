How To Run The Room

For Pedro only. This sheet is the choreography around the deck. The deck is what is on screen. The participant guide is what agents click. This is how you route five groups through one session without losing anyone.

The spine in one line Skill-routing opener, then one plain-language re-teach for the whole room, then five cohort exits. You teach the concepts once. The groups only differ at the open (which prompt) and the close (one action each).

Routing

Where Each Group Starts, Does, And Finishes

Read this before the session. During the live build, this is who is doing what. Do not force the editing group into Ads Manager.

01 · Not Started (recorded nothing)
During: follows the re-teach to learn the path. During the live build, runs the prompt to lock avatar and write the cold script instead of building.
Done: script in hand, recording booked this week
02 · Recorded, Not Edited (stuck editing)
During: sits through the re-teach, but the skill is NOT their tool today. The live build is not for them. Their job is to finish the edit. Say it plainly so they do not feel behind.
Done: one video edited, or a hard edit deadline set
03 · Recorded + Edited (video ready)
During: runs the prompt for ad copy, offer, ManyChat keyword. Builds the cold campaign in DRAFT so it is ready when they want to publish.
Done: ad copy + offer + keyword drafted, cold in draft
04 · Audiences Built (finished S6)
During: builds live with you, click for click. Fills the setup sheet via the prompt, then transfers it into the cold campaign and audiences.
Done: setup sheet filled, cold campaign + audiences in draft
05 · Ready For More (ahead)
During: runs the full build, refines targeting and creative, then becomes your floating helper for groups 3 and 4.
Done: campaign optimized, or a teammate unblocked
Routing rule of thumb "Build live with me if you have audiences. Build in draft if you have a video. Learn the path and run the prompt if you have neither. And if you are editing, you are excused from the laptop, go finish."
The Re-Teach

Teaching The 9 Concepts (slides 6 to 14)

Every concept the same shape: plain definition, then Marisol & Jay, then one action. Roughly 2 minutes each. Below is the beat to hit and the trap to avoid for each.

6
Teach · The Map

Three separate campaigns, exclusions cascade. This frames everything after it.

Land this

"Three campaigns, not one with three parts. Cram them into one and the same person sees all three ads and you pay three times."

7
Teach · 3 Floors

Campaign = building (goal). Ad set = floor (who, budget, where). Ad = room (video + words).

Land this

"Before you click anything, say out loud which floor you are on."

8
Teach · Objective

It tells Meta what human to find. Engagement, Leads, Conversions across the three campaigns.

Land this

"Wrong objective, wrong crowd, no matter how good the ad."

9
Teach · Housing SAC

Declared Housing, targeting locks, the copy does the targeting. This is the one that trips people, slow down.

Land this

"Generic fails, specific filters. If the ad works for anybody, it works for nobody."

10
Teach · Where Lead Lands

Message destinations, the conversation starts in DM with an auto-greeting.

Land this

"She taps at midnight, a friendly message starts the conversation while you sleep."

11
Teach · Audience Cascade

The one they built last week. Location + saved lists, exclude down the chain.

Land this

"You already built this. Lists first, before any ad set."

12
Teach · Budget

$20 to $50/day. 10% of target earnings. Small enough to learn, big enough for data.

Land this

"$280 over two weeks is not gambling, it is cheap tuition."

13
Teach · Placement

Advantage+ placements for 90% of them. Do not overthink it.

Land this

"Leave it on Advantage+ until you have a reason on paper to change it."

14
Teach · Metrics

Ignore likes. Cost per result, CTR, CPM, frequency, results. Headline: cost per conversation. Open your real dashboard here.

Land this

"A cheap lead who never replies is not cheap. An expensive lead who becomes a client is a bargain."

Pacing watch-out The re-teach is 9 slides in 20 minutes. If a concept sparks great questions, let it breathe and trim the live build, not the teach. The teach is the whole point of this session.
Watch-Outs

Where People Get Stuck, And What To Do

The editing-stuck group feels behind and tries to build anyway
Do: name it as a real win. "Your job today is one finished edit. That is progress and it counts. Do not open Ads Manager." Keep them in the re-teach, excuse them from the laptop.
Someone panics that targeting (age, ZIP, interests) is greyed out
Do: that is Housing working, not a bug. "Locked on purpose. The copy does the targeting." Move on, do not let it derail the room.
Confusion between campaign, ad set, and ad
Do: go back to the building metaphor. Building, floor, room. Have them point at which level Ads Manager is showing them right now.
They want to set the budget high to "go faster"
Do: repeat the tuition line. $20 to $50/day, leave it alone for the learning phase. High budget early just burns money before Meta learns.
An agent falls behind in the live build
Do: pause at the end of the current screen, not mid-field. "Catch me up, nobody gets left on a step." Drafts save automatically.
Group asks about Warm and Retarget builds
Do: the guide covers those by analogy (same clicks, change the objective and attach the audience). Tell them the screenshots for those are coming, today we nail Cold and the audiences.
Recruiting-minded agent asks why it says Housing
Do: "Selling homes is Housing. Recruiting agents is the one exception, that is Employment. Same lesson either way, the copy does the targeting."
Close

Before Anyone Leaves

  • Every agent can name their cohort
  • Every agent has said their one next action out loud
  • The scoreboard number is locked: cost per conversation
  • Group 1 has a recording booked this week
  • Group 2 has a hard edit deadline
  • Group 3 has cold built in draft
  • Group 4 has cold + audiences in draft
  • Group 5 has helped at least one teammate
Closing line "Wherever you walked in, you walk out one real step further, and you finally know what every button does. That was the whole point. Track your cost per conversation. See you next Tuesday."
