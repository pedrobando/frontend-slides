Concept 01 of 09

Campaign, Ad Set, Ad: Three Floors

What It Is

Campaign = the building. One decision: your goal.
Ad set = the floor. Who sees it, your budget, where it shows.
Ad = the room. The actual video and words.

For Marisol & Jay

Campaign goal: get conversations. Ad set: show it near Orlando for $20/day. Ad: the reel that says "I help first-time buyers near Orlando stop renting."

Do this: before you click, say out loud which floor you are on. Campaign, ad set, or ad.

Concept 02 of 09

Objective: What Human To Go Find

What It Is

The objective tells Meta who to hunt for. Engagement finds commenters. Leads finds form-fillers. Conversions finds bookers. Wrong pick, wrong crowd, no matter how good the ad.

For Marisol & Jay

Cold = Engagement (get her to comment). Warm = Leads (get her to raise her hand). Retarget = Conversions (get her to book the call).

Do this: match the objective to the next action you want, not the one you wish for.

Concept 03 of 09

Special Ad Category: Housing

What It Is

Real estate ads must be declared Housing. Meta then locks age, gender, and tight location. Do not panic. The copy does the targeting. Generic fails, specific filters.

For Marisol & Jay

"First-time buyers near Orlando, tired of renting" filters better than any age slider. If the ad works for anybody, it works for nobody.

Do this: declare Housing on step one, every time. Recruiting agents is the one exception (Employment).

Concept 04 of 09

Where The Lead Lands

What It Is

When someone taps, they go somewhere. We pick Message destinations (Messenger, Instagram, WhatsApp) so it becomes a real conversation, not a cold form. A small auto-greeting starts it for you.

For Marisol & Jay

She taps at midnight. A friendly message pops up: "Hi, are you renting right now?" She answers. Now you are talking to a real buyer, not chasing an abandoned form.

Do this: pick where the conversation happens before you build the ad. Wire the greeting so no tap gets silence.

Concept 05 of 09 · You Built This Last Week

Audience + The Exclusion Cascade

What It Is

Under Housing you keep two levers: a location and saved lists of people by what they did (watched, engaged, messaged). Cold excludes warm, warm excludes leads, retarget excludes bookers. No one sees the same ad twice.

For Marisol & Jay

First time: a stranger in Orlando. Second time: a watcher. Third time: an almost. Each campaign excludes the stage before it, so your money hits fresh eyes.

Do this: build your lists first, before any ad set. Exactly like Session 6.

Concept 06 of 09

Budget: Small Enough To Learn

What It Is

$20 to $50 a day per campaign. Small enough that a bad week does not hurt, big enough that Meta can learn. Big-picture rule: plan to spend about 10% of what you want to earn.

For Marisol & Jay

$20/day on cold for two weeks is $280 to find out if your message lands. That is not gambling. It is cheap tuition.

Do this: pick a daily number you will not flinch at, then leave it alone long enough to learn.

Concept 07 of 09

Placement: Where It Shows Up

What It Is

Placement is where your ad appears (reels, feed, stories). Two roads: Advantage+ placements lets Meta choose, right for 90% of you. Manual is hand-picking, only once data says one spot wins.

For Marisol & Jay

Your reel was shot vertical for phones. Let Meta run it everywhere and it pours into reels and stories, where she scrolls at night.

Do this: leave it on Advantage+ placements until you have a reason on paper to change it.

Concept 08 + 09 of 09 · Your Scoreboard

The Numbers That Actually Matter

Meta shows a hundred numbers. Most are noise. Ignore likes. Read these.

Cost / Result
What you pay each time someone does the thing you wanted.
Click Rate (CTR)
Are people tapping, or scrolling past?
Cost / 1,000 (CPM)
How expensive your audience is to reach.
Frequency
Times one person saw it. Past 3 to 4 on retarget, refresh.
Results
Comments, leads, or bookings, per the objective.
Cost / Conversation
Your headline number: dollars to get one real buyer talking.
Do this: after today, read only these numbers and write down your cost per conversation. Live demo: open your dashboard
