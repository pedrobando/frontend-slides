Before The Frameworks

Feel It Before You Write It

Question 1

What is my avatar doing at 11pm that they shouldn’t have to be doing?

“Refreshing email waiting for a deal that never came.”

Question 2

What have they tried that didn’t work?

“KW training they stopped attending. eXp Zoom calls nobody showed up for.”

Question 3 — The Gold

What are they afraid to admit out loud?

“I’m almost 30 and I still don’t know if real estate is actually going to work for me.”

→ Write from Question 3. That’s the hook.

Framework 1 of 6

AIDA — Attention · Interest · Desire · Action

The classic funnel framework. Best for cold audiences who don’t know you yet. Top of funnel.

AttentionInterestDesireAction
Pedro’s Avatar — Full Example
Attention

“You closed 8 deals this year. Your broker collected their split. And you still don’t know where deal 9 is coming from.”

Interest

“Most agents producing $1M–$3M are one system away from scaling. But nobody ever sits down and builds it with them.”

Desire

“At reThought, agents train live every Tuesday — marketing, production, prospecting, analytics. With a broker who picks up the phone. In two markets.”

Action

“Book 20 minutes with Pedro. No pitch. No pressure. Just an honest conversation about where you’re going.”

Framework 2 of 6

PAS — Problem · Agitate · Solution

Best for warm audiences — people who know they have a problem. Pain-aware. Mid funnel.

Pedro’s Avatar — PAS Example
Problem

“You’re producing $1M–$3M a year and you’re still winging it every single month.”

Agitate

“No pipeline system. No real training. A broker who collects splits and disappears. You’re working harder than ever and you still can’t predict where next month’s check is coming from.”

Solution

“reThought was built for exactly this. Live weekly training. A 48-week curriculum. A broker who actually picks up the phone. Two markets. One community.”

→ The agitate section is where most agents pull back because they feel mean. Don’t. If you’re truly describing your avatar’s reality, it’s not mean — it’s accurate.

Framework 3 of 6

FAB — Feature · Advantage · Benefit

Best for product-aware audiences comparing options. They know they need something — they’re deciding between you and someone else.

Pedro’s Avatar — FAB Example
Feature

“reThought runs live training every Tuesday — marketing, production, prospecting, and analytics.”

Advantage

“Unlike pre-recorded courses or monthly Zoom calls with 200 people, this is a small-group session where you get direct feedback on YOUR business.”

Benefit

“You stop guessing what to do next. You show up Tuesday, you leave with a plan, and you execute the rest of the week with clarity.”

Framework 4 of 6

SPIN — Situation · Problem · Implication · Need

Consultative framework. Best for 1-on-1 conversations, DMs, coffee meetings, and longer-form content.

Pedro’s Avatar — SPIN Example
Situation

“You closed 8 deals last year at a $2M volume per deal.”

Problem

“But you don’t have a repeatable pipeline. Every deal feels like you’re starting from scratch.”

Implication

“If nothing changes, you’ll close 8 again next year. Maybe 6. And you’ll burn out because the hustle doesn’t scale.”

Need-Payoff

“What if you had a system that generated 3–5 qualified conversations per week — without cold calling? That’s what we build at reThought.”

Framework 5 of 6

SCIPAB — The Complete Framework

Six steps. Best for long-form content — emails, landing pages, video scripts, pitch decks.

01 Situation
02 Complication
03 Implication
04 Position
05 Action
06 Benefit
Pedro’s Avatar — SCIPAB Example
Situation

“You’re a producing agent. $1M–$3M in annual volume. You’re not new — you’ve got skills.”

Complication

“But your brokerage treats you like a number. No real training. No marketing help. No accountability.”

Implication

“Without a system, you’ll keep grinding at the same level. In 2 years, you’re either out or back at a desk job.”

Position

“reThought is a production-centric boutique firm. We don’t recruit 200 agents. We develop 20 — deeply.”

Action

“Book a 20-minute call. See the training calendar. Meet the community.”

Benefit

“You leave the call with clarity — whether you join or not.”

Framework 6 of 6

IDCA — Interest · Desire · Conviction · Action

Skips attention — your audience already knows you. Best for retargeting and warm audiences who haven’t acted yet.

Pedro’s Avatar — IDCA Example
Interest

“You’ve been watching our training clips. You’ve seen our agents post their wins. Something about it keeps pulling you back.”

Desire

“You want what they have — a team that actually trains together, a broker who builds alongside you, and a system that doesn’t depend on luck.”

Conviction

“Last quarter, our agents averaged 3 closings each. Not because they’re superhuman — because they follow a system. The same system you’d get on day one.”

Action

“Stop watching from the sideline. Book 20 minutes. Let’s see if this is the right fit.”

Quick Reference

All 6 Frameworks at a Glance

Framework Best For Tone Funnel Stage
AIDA Cold audiences, first impressions Bold, attention-grabbing Top
PAS Pain-aware audiences Empathetic, confrontational Mid
FAB Product-aware, comparing options Clear, logical Mid–Bottom

SCIPAB Long-form: emails, scripts, decks Thorough, persuasive Full funnel
IDCA Retargeting, warm followers Familiar, proof-driven Bottom
