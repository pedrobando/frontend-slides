## Module 00 — Why This Appendix Exists / Por Qué Existe Este Apéndice

### Coach Note from the Broker

We have run the system long enough to know that the part agents skip first is the taxonomy. They claim a lead, run the cadence, and forget to set the stage or apply the tags. The result is a database where "hot" means nothing because everyone defines it differently, and "long-nurture" is a graveyard because no one tagged the leads who actually fit the cadence. This appendix locks the vocabulary. Once locked, the scoreboard becomes honest. Once honest, the conversations during Wednesday training become surgical instead of vague.

### The Big Idea

Two fields in Lofty carry the entire weight of the system: **Stage** and **Tags**.

- **Stage** tells you where the lead is in time.
- **Tags** tell you who the lead is and what they want.

Without both, every other system in this playbook is unmeasurable.

Stage is one value per lead. Tags are multiple. Stage drives the action plan. Tags drive the segmentation, the drip selection, and the filter the agent uses every morning to decide who gets called first.

### What You Owe the Team This Cycle
1. Read this appendix cover to cover before Wednesday of Week 02.
2. Audit your current Lofty database against the 11 stages.
3. Apply at least 2 tags to every lead you have claimed in the last 90 days.
4. Bring one question to Wednesday Week 03 about a lead you are not sure how to tag.

**HARD RULE:** From this cycle forward, no lead claim is complete until stage is set and at least two tags are applied. The 24-hour install metric includes both.

## Module 01 — The 11 Stages / Las 11 Etapas

### THE BIG IDEA

Every lead exists at exactly one stage at any moment. The stage is the lead's relationship to a transaction in time. It is not how much you like them. It is not how recently they replied. It is the answer to one question: **Where is this person in the buy or sell timeline right now?**

### 1.1 The 11 Stages

| # | Stage | Definition | Exit Criteria |
|---|-------|-----------|---------------|
| 01 | Lead | New, not reached yet. Still in the contact-attempt loop. | Voice contact + qualify timeline, OR 10 phone attempts over 14 days with no answer → drop to C-Cold or Contact. |
| 02 | A. Hot | Buying or selling in less than 3 months. Pre-approved or near it. | → Active Client when agreement signed. → B-Warm if timeline extends past 3 months. Never backward to Lead. |
| 03 | B. Warm | Buying or selling in 3 to 6 months. Researching, pre-qualifying. | → A-Hot when timeline tightens. → C-Cold if no engagement for 45 days. |
| 04 | C. Cold | Buying or selling in 6+ months. Long-nurture flow. | → B-Warm or A-Hot on engagement signals. Never delete without broker approval. |
| 05 | Active Client | Signed buyer or seller agreement. Currently working a transaction. | → Pending when offer accepted. → A-Hot if agreement expires without closing. |
| 06 | Pending | Offer accepted. Under contract. Waiting on close. | → Past Client at closing. → Active Client if contract terminates. |
| 07 | Past Client | Closed a transaction. Stay in touch. | Stays here permanently unless they re-engage. |
| 08 | Sphere | Personal relationship. Family, close friends, mentors, NAHREP peers. | Stays here. Different drip than Past Client. Annual/holiday/birthday touches only. |
| 09 | Contact | No longer pursuing. Already has an agent, not interested, out of area. | No drips, no calls. Re-engagement only if lead initiates. |
| 10 | Closed | Past client you do not want to stay in contact with. | Stays here. Requires broker note in Lofty. Used sparingly. |
| 11 | Trash | Bad data, fake submission, spam, or duplicate. | Reviewed quarterly by broker before permanent delete. |

### 1.2 How Stage Drives Lofty

Every stage maps to a Lofty action plan. When you change the stage, the action plan auto-applies.

- **Lead:** 10-attempt phone cadence + Day 0 voicemail and email + Day 2 follow-up + Day 7 market report.
- **A-Hot:** Weekly personal touch + biweekly market report + Pre-Pitch trigger if price threshold met.
- **B-Warm:** Biweekly personal touch + monthly market report + quarterly check-in call.
- **C-Cold:** Monthly market report + quarterly pressure-on-yourself follow-up + semi-annual personal touch.
- **Active Client:** Transaction-driven communication. No drips. Custom cadence per deal stage.
- **Pending:** Transaction milestone touches (inspection, appraisal, financing, clear-to-close, walk-through, closing).
- **Past Client:** Quarterly value touch + annual closing-anniversary note + market report tied to their property's geography.
- **Sphere:** Holiday touch + birthday touch + annual personal note. No market reports unless requested.
- **Contact and Closed:** No drips. No touches. Locked out of all automated communication.

### 1.3 Movement Rules

**Always forward, rarely back.** Stages only move backward in three specific scenarios:
1. Active Client → A-Hot if the agreement expires without close.
2. Pending → Active Client if a contract dies.
3. C-Cold → B-Warm or A-Hot if engagement signals fire.

Outside those three cases, do not move a lead backward.

**The 45-Day Silence Rule:** Any lead in A-Hot or B-Warm with no engagement in 45 days drops one stage automatically. A-Hot → B-Warm. B-Warm → C-Cold.

**The 7-Day Signal Rule:** Any lead in C-Cold who opens a market report 3+ times in 7 days, replies to a drip, or visits the site twice in a week moves up to B-Warm.

**HARD RULE — NEVER DELETE FROM C-COLD.** C-Cold is where the highest-margin transactions hide. Long-nurture conversion rates often exceed fresh-lead conversion. Never delete without explicit broker approval and a written reason logged in Lofty.

## Module 02 — The Tag System / El Sistema de Etiquetas

### THE BIG IDEA

Stage answers "when." Tags answer "who, what, where, and how." A lead in A-Hot tells you to call them this week. A lead in A-Hot tagged Spanish, Luxury Buyer, Lake Nona, NAHREP-Network tells you to call them this week in Spanish, lead with a Pre-Pitch, send Lake Nona comparables, and reference your shared NAHREP community.

### 2.1 Tag Categories

Tags split into four categories. Every lead carries at least one tag from the first two.

1. **Persona tags** / the WHO. One per lead. Always.
2. **Specialty tags** / the WHAT. One per lead when applicable.
3. **Cultural and Network tags** / the HOW. Applied when applicable.
4. **Market tags** / the WHERE. Applied when primary market identified.

### 2.2 Persona Tags (one per lead, always)

| Tag | When to Apply |
|-----|---------------|
| Buyer | Looking to purchase. No sale of existing home required. |
| Seller | Looking to sell. May or may not be buying after. |
| Buyer/Seller | Dual transaction. Must sell to buy. Timing coordination is the value. |
| Luxury Buyer | Above the Pre-Pitch price threshold. Automatic Pre-Pitch trigger. |
| Luxury Seller | Property above the Pre-Pitch threshold. Different listing cadence. |
| Luxury Buyer/Seller | Both sides above luxury threshold. Highest-margin profile. Broker-led. |

### 2.3 Specialty Tags (when applicable)

| Tag | When to Apply |
|-----|---------------|
| Expired | Previous listing expired or withdrawn unsold. Tied to expired-listing call script. |
| FSBO | Currently selling without representation. Tied to FSBO conversion script. |
| New-Construction | Focused on new-build communities. Different comp logic, 6-18 month timeline. |
| STR-Investor | Short-term rental investor. Cash-on-cash calculation drives decision. CDD fees, HOA rules, STR licensing. |
| Investor | Long-term rental or flipper. Not STR. Different financing and exit calculation. |
| First-Time-Buyer | Never purchased a primary residence. Heavier education cadence. |
| Veteran | VA-eligible. Different financing path. Veteran-specific welcome drip + lender referral. |
| Relocation-In | Moving into our markets from out of state/country. Concierge service. |
| Relocation-Out | Selling here, moving away. Outbound referral opportunity. |

### 2.4 Cultural and Network Tags (when applicable)

| Tag | When to Apply |
|-----|---------------|
| Spanish | Prefers Spanish for communication. All defaults to Spanish first, English second. |
| Bilingual | Comfortable in both languages. Code-switching appreciated. Both languages in cadence. |
| NAHREP-Network | Came from NAHREP referral, event, or affiliation. Different opener script + testimonial bank. |
| Cross-Border | Buying or selling across national/territorial border. PR, Mexico, Caribbean. Bilingual closing complexity. |
| Referral | Came from Past Client or Sphere introduction. Highest conversion rate. Different first-touch script. |
| Sphere-Referral | Referral from family, mentor, NAHREP peer. Highest-trust origin. Thank referrer within 24 hours. |

### 2.5 Market Tags (one per lead, always)

| Market Tag | Coverage |
|-----------|----------|
| Central-Florida | Orlando metro umbrella. Use when lead has not narrowed to specific community. |
| Lake-Nona | Lake Nona Central, Laureate Park, Northlake Park, Medical City corridor. |
| Doctor-Phillips | Doctor Phillips, Windermere, Bay Hill, Restaurant Row corridor. |
| Reunion-Storey-Champions | STR and vacation-home market. Reunion Resort, Storey Lake, Champions Gate, Solara, Encore. |
| Miami-Urban | Brickell, Downtown Miami, Edgewater, Coral Gables, Coconut Grove. |
| Tampa-Bay | South Tampa, Hyde Park, Davis Islands, Channelside. Flood-zone + insurance complexity. |
| Milwaukee-Metro | Brookfield, Mequon, Wauwatosa, Shorewood, Whitefish Bay, North Shore corridor. |
| Puerto-Rico | San Juan metro, Dorado, Condado, Palmas del Mar. Cross-border tag almost always applies. |

**HARD RULE — TWO TAGS MINIMUM:** Every lead carries at minimum one Persona tag and one Market tag. Without both, the lead is invisible to segmentation. The 24-hour install metric includes tag application. No exceptions.

## Module 03 — How Tags Drive Cadence / Cómo las Etiquetas Manejan la Cadencia

### THE BIG IDEA

Tags exist so that one filter, applied in the morning, returns the exact list of leads who get the exact cadence today. The agent who runs the system right does not think "who should I call." They open Lofty, filter by tag, and call the list.

### 3.1 Daily Filter Examples

| Filter Combination | What It Returns | Cadence to Run |
|-------------------|----------------|----------------|
| A-Hot + Spanish | Hot leads who prefer Spanish | Weekly Spanish-first call. Bilingual market report. |
| Luxury Buyer + Lake-Nona | Lake Nona buyers above Pre-Pitch threshold | Pre-Pitch template. Mailed physical at $1M+. |
| STR-Investor + Reunion-Storey-Champions | STR investors in Disney corridor | STR rule brief. CDD fee analysis. Cash-on-cash worksheet. |
| C-Cold + Past Client (referrer) | Cold long-nurture from past clients | Quarterly pressure-on-yourself follow-up. Loop original referrer. |
| Relocation-In + Puerto-Rico | Buyers moving from PR to mainland | Bilingual relocation brief. PR-side seller referral. Cross-border timing call. |
| Seller + Brookfield (or any market) | Sellers in a specific market | Listing-presentation prep. CMA pull. Seller pre-pitch. |
| NAHREP-Network + Buyer | NAHREP-tied buyers across all stages | Community-first script. NAHREP testimonial bank. |
| Expired + Last 90 days | Recent expired listings | Expired listing recovery script. Listing diagnostic offer. |

### 3.2 The Morning Call List

Every morning, in this order. This is the order that converts.

1. **List 1. New Leads** (Lead stage). Speed-to-lead window. 1 minute from claim.
2. **List 2. Active Client + Pending.** Transaction-driven touches.
3. **List 3. A-Hot.** Weekly cadence calls due today.
4. **List 4. B-Warm.** Biweekly cadence calls due today.
5. **List 5. Past Client + Sphere.** Anniversary, birthday, value touches due today.
6. **List 6. C-Cold.** Pressure-on-yourself follow-up if signals fire.

**WHY THIS ORDER:** Speed-to-lead comes first because the window closes fastest. Active deals second because they are closest revenue. Then the temperature gradient. C-Cold is bonus, not the main event.

## Module 05 — Hygiene, Maintenance, Audit

### THE BIG IDEA

A taxonomy is only useful if it stays clean. Three rules protect the system from drift.

### 5.1 Weekly Hygiene
- **Stage check.** Every Friday, review every lead in A-Hot and B-Warm. Verify the 45-day silence rule demotions.
- **Tag completeness.** Run filter for any lead with fewer than 2 tags. Count should be zero by Friday.
- **Inbox to zero.** Lofty inbox, today's tasks, and overdue tasks all end the week at zero.

### 5.2 Monthly Audit
- **C-Cold engagement scan.** Pull every C-Cold lead with 3+ market report opens in last 30 days. Run the 7-day signal rule. Move to B-Warm.
- **Persona tag audit.** Filter for Buyer tags older than 6 months. Many have become Buyer/Seller or Past Client. Refresh.
- **Trash review.** Pull Trash stage. Confirm with broker. If unclear, leave them.

### 5.3 Quarterly Refresh
- **Tag taxonomy review.** New market or persona in last 90 days? Bring proposed tags to Q-end retreat. Broker approves.
- **Stage conversion math.** Calculate: Lead-to-A-Hot rate. A-Hot-to-Active-Client rate. Active-Client-to-Pending rate. Pending-to-Past-Client rate. These are the team's true conversion funnel.
- **Permanent deletes.** Broker reviews Trash stage. Anything ambiguous moves to Contact.

**HARD RULE — THE AUDIT IS NOT OPTIONAL.** Weekly hygiene: 20 minutes. Monthly audit: 90 minutes. Quarterly refresh: 3 hours at the retreat. The agents who skip these are the agents whose pipelines "mysteriously" stop converting.
